Outreach and Teaching

What good is knowing things if you can't get that information out?

Teaching and outreach is very important to me. It's one thing to be good at science and math and all this other stuff, but it does no good to keep all the goodies to myself. I really enjoy teaching, so these are a couple of examples of the teaching and outreach I do.

MGLC Transferable Skills Workshop

I am an active member in Northwestern University's McCormick Graduate Leadership Council (MGLC), and am the primary organizer for our Transferable Skills Workshops. A friend and I started these workshops back in 2013, and since then I and other instructors have run workshops for over 600 graduate students.

In particular, I created materials for and teach the workshops on MATLAB and SolidWorks. You can download workshop materials using the buttons in the sidebar.

CBM Outreach

Our research lab requires students to present our research and demonstrate some of the work we do at high school and younger schools at least once a year. In addition to this, I regularly participate in STEM outreach events at the Chicago Museum of Science and Industry, Adler Planetarium, and other STEM events throughout Chicago.
